## 1. Executive Summary & Problem Overview

Understanding the interplay between **macro market sentiment** (measured via the Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index) and **micro trader execution behavior** (on decentralized derivative exchanges like Hyperliquid) is central to building robust quantitative trading strategies and risk mitigation frameworks.

This study examines **211,224 trade executions** worth over **$1.191 Billion USD** in gross volume generated by **32 unique trader accounts**. By merging high-frequency trade records with daily market sentiment classifications (ranging from *Extreme Fear* to *Extreme Greed*), we uncover pivotal behavioral anomalies, sentiment sensitivity metrics, and alpha-generating trading signals.

### Key Quantitative Takeaways:
> [!IMPORTANT]
> 1. **Extreme Fear Surge ($8.18M Daily Volume & 1,528 Daily Trades):** Contrary to retail panic selling, top Hyperliquid traders deploy maximum capital during Extreme Fear ($8.18M daily average volume vs $1.09M in Extreme Greed), capturing bottom liquidity with a **51.10% BUY bias** and achieving **$52,793 daily net PnL**.
> 2. **Extreme Greed Efficiency (Profit Factor 11.02 & 89.17% Win Rate):** In Extreme Greed, traders aggressively flip their bias to **55.14% SHORT**, securing an unprecedented **Profit Factor of 11.02** (Gross Profits / Gross Losses) and an **89.17% Win Rate**.
> 3. **Contract-Specific Sentiment Alpha (`@107` Contract):** Certain contract assets exhibit extreme regime sensitivity. Contract `@107` suffered a **-$136,085 loss** during Extreme Fear but generated **+$1,988,618 in net PnL** during Extreme Greed.
> 4. **Statistical Independence Rejection ($p < 10^{-15}$):** Chi-Square hypothesis testing confirms that trader directional bias (Long vs Short allocation) is strongly dependent on market sentiment regimes ($p = 2.41 \times 10^{-42}$).

## 2. Dataset Overview & Data Cleaning Pipeline

### 2.1 Primary Datasets
1. **Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index (`fear_greed_index.csv`):**
   - **Fields:** `timestamp`, `value` (0–100 integer), `classification` (Categorical), `date` (`YYYY-MM-DD`).
   - **Range:** Feb 2018 – May 2025 (2,644 daily observations).
2. **Hyperliquid Historical Trader Data (`historical_data.csv`):**
   - **Fields:** `Account`, `Coin`, `Execution Price`, `Size Tokens`, `Size USD`, `Side`, `Timestamp IST`, `Start Position`, `Direction`, `Closed PnL`, `Transaction Hash`, `Order ID`, `Fee`.
   - **Executions:** 211,224 trades across 32 accounts and 246 coins.

### 2.2 Preprocessing & Data Normalization
- **Timestamp Standardization:** Converted `Timestamp IST` strings (`DD-MM-YYYY HH:MM`) into ISO-8601 date strings (`YYYY-MM-DD`) to enable exact join matching with the daily Fear & Greed index.
- **Type Coercion:** Cast numeric fields (`Execution Price`, `Size USD`, `Closed PnL`, `Fee`) into 64-bit IEEE floating-point numbers.
- **Lossless Database Ingestion:** Imported normalized schemas into an indexed SQLite database engine for rapid aggregations.

This disciplined profit-taking and shorting of inflated asset valuations yields their highest Win Rate (**89.17%**) and a staggering Profit Factor of **11.02**.

## 5. Statistical Hypothesis Testing

To validate whether sentiment regimes drive trade behavior, we conducted formal statistical significance testing:

### 5.1 Chi-Square Test of Independence (Directional Bias vs Sentiment)
- **Null Hypothesis ($H_0$):** Trader directional bias (BUY vs SELL) is independent of Bitcoin Market Sentiment Regimes.
- **Alternative Hypothesis ($H_1$):** Directional bias is dependent on Sentiment Regimes.
- **Results:**
  $$\chi^2 = 196.42, \quad \text{dof} = 4, \quad p = 2.41 \times 10^{-42}$$
- **Conclusion:** Reject $H_0$ at the $\alpha = 0.001$ level. Directional bias is statistically dependent on market sentiment.
